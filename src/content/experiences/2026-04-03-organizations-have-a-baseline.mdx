I keep coming back to the idea of a baseline. When I look at an organization, I do not just see features. I see the ground it builds on: the operating system, the platform, the stack, and the patterns that shape how the company works.

In some places that baseline is a Kubernetes stack. In others it might be an event-driven platform or a .NET and SQL Server shop. The point is not the exact technology. The point is that the baseline sets the shape of hiring, maintenance, and day-to-day engineering.

Everything on top of that baseline can change more easily. That is why big organizations often have multiple baselines inside them. Each team or section has its own ground, even if the company looks unified from the outside.
