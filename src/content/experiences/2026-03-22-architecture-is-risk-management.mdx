I have come to see architecture as a risk-management exercise. When I make a decision, I am usually asking what the risk is, what the reward is, and whether the team will be able to execute on it safely.

That makes me conservative by default. I would rather choose stability than take a leap of faith I cannot defend if things go wrong.

There may be cases where the reward is worth the risk, but I have not usually been pushed into that frame of mind. Most of the time I am trying to keep the decision explainable, durable, and safe for the team that has to live with it.
