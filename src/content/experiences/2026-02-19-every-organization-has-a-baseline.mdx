I keep noticing that organizations have a baseline. It is the ground they build on: the operating system, the platform, the stack, and the habits that shape the company.

Sometimes that baseline is a Kubernetes cluster. Sometimes it is a .NET and SQL Server shop. Sometimes it is an event-driven setup. The exact technology matters less than the fact that it becomes the reference point for how the company hires, maintains systems, and makes decisions.

I think that is why bigger companies often feel like they have multiple baselines. Different teams carry different assumptions, but they still sit on their own version of the same ground.
