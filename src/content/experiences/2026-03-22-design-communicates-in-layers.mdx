I have learned that it is hard to communicate design clearly when the organization and the system do not line up the same way. The easiest fix is often to talk through the model together and break it into parts.

When I write something down, order matters. I usually want to start with the simplest business flow first, then add the extra layers one at a time. If the business thinks in an idea-to-cash path, then the design should respect that order instead of starting from the part that is easiest for me technically.

That way, the next layer makes sense because the basic one is already in place. The complex version becomes a follow-up instead of a wall.
