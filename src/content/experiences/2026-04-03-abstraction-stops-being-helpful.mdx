I keep thinking about abstraction as something that should help, not hide the cost of change. A simple to-do app is a good example: it is easy to keep abstracting until even a small change takes more than a couple lines of code.

That is where abstraction stops being useful to me. If I have to keep asking "what does this really do?" before I can make a tiny change, then the design has probably gone too far.

I still like the experiment, though. It is a useful way to see where flexibility turns into overhead and where a clean system starts becoming an open-ended engineering project.
