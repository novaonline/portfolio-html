Documenting your thought process is an ability I admire in tech leads and architects. When I can show how I arrived at a decision, it is easier for people to trust that it is coming from the right state of mind, and it opens the door to healthy debate.

Before I send a document for review, I want it to have already gone through my own first or second pass of correcting myself. GenAI can help me catch blind spots I might have missed, but I still use my own judgment when something sounds off, and I may reject AI feedback if it feels like a hallucination. Finally, I know the outcome can still have gaps because of the organization's constraints. That is why it is important to have a second or third pair of eyes from colleagues, especially one that the document or decision directly affects.
